POLICY SHIFTS ‚Äî Trade Liberalisation & Global Context

Tariff Structure Over Time

Reveals how tariff reductions and free-trade policies transformed Asia‚Äôs trade landscape over the past two decades..

Key Insights

  • Duty-free tariff lines represent goods that enter a country without import taxes. Hong Kong and Singapore show 100% duty-free rates as they operate free-port systems with zero import tariffs.
  • Dutiable tariff lines indicate goods that still face import duties. These are darker shades such as South Asian and Central Asian economies.
  • Specific tariff lines are fixed-amount duties (e.g., $10 per tonne of rice) These are rare in Asia, appearing mainly for agricultural or sensitive goods.

Impact of New U.S. Tariffs

Relevance of Tarrifs today - How recent U.S. tariff changes affect developing and vulnerable economies.

Key Insights

  • Major economies such as the EU, UK, and Japan benefit from lower average tariffs through special agreements, while vulnerable economies are left behind.
  • Least developed countries like Myanmar, Lao PDR, and Bangladesh face the steepest tariff increases, reducing their competitiveness in U.S. markets.
  • Enhanced cooperation is essential ‚Äî vulnerable economies should seek exemptions, diversify exports, and strengthen regional trade partnerships.

HISTORICAL TRENDS ‚Äî Trade Openness Over Time

Historical Insight

Trade openness in Asia has evolved significantly since the late 1980s. Most economies began liberalising trade during the 1990s, with export-to-GDP ratios rising sharply following globalisation and regional integration through ASEAN and APEC frameworks.

The 2008 global financial crisis caused a temporary contraction, but recovery was strong, particularly in East and Southeast Asia. In recent years, openness has stabilised, with emerging economies diversifying into high-value exports.
